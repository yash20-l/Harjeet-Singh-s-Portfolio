My Journey

My Story

is bright & will be

Reflecting on my journey into filmmaking. It all began when I was a teenager, and my imagination started to take flight. I vividly envisioned scenes in my mind, and a deep desire to attend film schools filled me with excitement. But fate took me to that boarding school in Lion Batisfec, and the melancholy and glumness engulfed me.
I wish my parents understood my need for freshness and life experiences, but financial burdens seemed to consume us, pushing us too far. Then, when I was just 13, my grandfather passed away, leaving us devastated. His loss hit me like a ton of bricks, and before we could recover, more problems kept pouring in. I had to juggle my studies while dealing with our financial struggles. It was tough, but amidst it all, my love for films and books kept me going.
Finally, I completed my secondary education, but it came at the cost of selling our belongings. It was a challenging time, but I didn't lose sight of my passion for filmmaking. When my maternal uncle expressed his desire to produce a movie based on Sectism, I saw an opportunity. "Rupees of God" became my first film, and its success brought me immense joy and pride.
I couldn't resist experimenting with more films like "Rupees of God," which gained recognition but also posed new challenges. Finding producers for my future projects was tough, and despite creating meaningful films like "Caution" for children's safety, support seemed elusive. I hoped those who appreciated my work would come forward to help, but it didn't materialize.
Still, I won't give up. I believe in the power of my passion and creativity, and I'll keep pushing forward, overcoming whatever comes my way. Filmmaking is my calling, and I'll continue telling stories that touch hearts and make a difference.
